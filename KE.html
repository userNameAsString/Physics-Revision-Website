Kinetic Energy

Kinetic energy is the energy of movement.

The kinetic energy of a moving object can be calculated using the equation:

kinetic energy = 0.5 × mass × speed2

Ek = ½mv2

recall and apply

kinetic energy, Ek , in joules, J

mass, m, in kilograms, kg

speed, v, in metres per second, m/s

Rearrange to find:

mass - m = 2Ek/v2

speed - v = √2E/m

Worked example

Calculate the speed of a 350 g ball with 70 joules of kinetic energy

350g = 0.35kg

Ek = ½mv2

70 = ½*0.35*v2

v2 = 140/0.35

v2 = 400

v = 20m/s
