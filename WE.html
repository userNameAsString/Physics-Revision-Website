Weight Equation

Weight is the force acting on an object and is not the same as mass

Mass is a measure of how difficult it is to change the velocity of an object

The weight an object has can be calculated using the equation:

weight = mass × gravitational field strength

W = mg

recall and apply

weight, W, in newtons, N

mass, m, in kilograms, kg

gravitational field strength, g, in newtons per kilogram, N/kg

Rearrange to find:

mass - m = W/g

gravitational field strength - g = W/h

Worked example

Calculate the weight of a 1500 g rock on the surface of the Earth.(Use g = 10N/kg)

1500g = 1.5kg

W = mg

W = 1.5 x 10

W = 15 N
