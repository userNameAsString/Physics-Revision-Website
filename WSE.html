Wave Speed Equation

Wave speed is the speed at which energy is transferred through a medium and changes depending on the density of said medium.

The speed of a wave can be calculated using the equation:

wave speed = frequency * wavelength

v = fλ

recall and apply

wave speed, v, in metres per second, m/s

frequency, f, in hertz, Hz

wavelength, λ, in metres, m

Fun fact: According to 9a3 this is the best equation

Rearrange to find:

frequency - f = v/λ

wavelength - λ = v/f

Worked example

Calculate the frequency of red light. The wavelength of red light is 6.0 x 10-7 m and the speed of electromagnetic waves is 3.0 x 108 m/s.

v = fλ

3*108 = f*6*10-7

f = 5*1014Hz

f = 500THz
