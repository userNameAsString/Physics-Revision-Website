Transferring Energy

Energy can be transferred from one store to another, by processes such as: 

  • Heating
  • Light and sound waves
  • Electric currents
  • Chemical reactions
  • Mechanical work - a force moving an object through a distance 

The Principle of Conservation of Energy 

Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred from one type into other types. 

When one energy store empties, another energy store elsewhere must be filling. The energy is just shifted from store to store. Energy is not used up, but is conserved. 

Example

An example would be combining chemicals (food and oxygen) to lift an ornament up onto a shelf, but raising this ornament also sets up another kind of store.

This store involves the ornament positioned higher in the Earth's gravitational field. From the energy perspective, this event is seen in the following way: 

Energy is shifted from the chemical store of your muscles to the gravitational potential store of the ornament in the Earth's gravitational field. 

The energy is shifted from one store to another because a force does work on the object.
