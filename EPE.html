Elastic Potential Energy

A force that stretches (or compresses) a spring does work and elastic potential energy is stored in the spring.

The work done in stretching (or compressing) a spring (up to the limit of proportionality) is calculated using the equation:

elastic potential energy = 0.5 × spring constant × extension2

Ee = ½ke2

select and apply

elastic potential energy, Ee, in joules, J

spring constant, k, in newtons per metre, N/m

extension, e, in metres, m

Rearrange to find:

spring constant - k = 2Ee/e2

extension - e = √2E/k
