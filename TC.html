Thermal Conduction

What is thermal conduction? 

A transfer process/pathway which moves energy from one thermal store to another i.e. from one place to another. Thermal conduction is how thermal energy moves through solids.


For thermal conduction there is no equation.

You need to know that:

  • Thermal conduction is how energy moves through solids.
  • The higher the thermal conductivity of a material the higher the rate of energy transfer by conduction across the material. 
  • The rate of cooling of a building is affected by the thickness and thermal conductivity of its walls.
