Specific Latent Heat

The specific latent heat of a substance is the amount of energy required to change the state of one kilogram of the substance with no change in temperature.

Specific latent heat of fusion – energy needed to change the state of solid to a liquid.

Specific latent heat of vaporisation – energy needed to change the state of liquid to a gas.

The energy used to change the state of a substance can be calculated with the equation:

energy = mass * specific latent heat

E = mL
