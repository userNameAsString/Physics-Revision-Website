Average Acceleration Equation

An object is accelerating when its velocity changes.

This means that an object is also accelerating when it changes direction(because its velocity would change).

The average acceleration of an object can be calculated using the equation:

acceleration = change in velocity/time taken

a = Δv/t

acceleration = final speed - starting speed/time taken

a = v-u/t

recall and apply

acceleration, a, in metres per second squared, m/s2

change in speed, Δv, in metres per second, m/s

final speed, v, in metres per second, m/s

starting speed, u, in metres per second, m/s

time, t, in seconds, s

Note: Both equations are the same but the second one is personally easier to use

Rearrange to find:

change in speed - Δv = at

time - t = v-u/a or t = Δv/a

final speed - v = at + u

starting speed - u = v - at
