Gravitational Potential Energy

Gravitational potential energy is the energy gained when an object is raised.

The amount of gravitational potential energy an object has can be calculated using the equation:

g.p.e . = mass × gravitational field strength × height

Ep = mgh

recall and apply

energy, Ep, in joules, J

mass, m, in kilograms, kg

gravitational field strength, g, in newtons per kilogram, N/kg

height, h, in metres, m

Note: The gravitational field strength will always be given in the question

Rearrange to find:

mass - m = Ep/gh

gravitational field strength - g = Ep/mh

height - h = Ep/mg

Worked example

A 3500g object gains 2100 J of gravitational potential energy when it is raised to a height, h. Calculate the height of the object. (g = 10 N/kg)

3500g = 3.5kg

Ep = mgh

2100 = 3.5*10*h

2100/35 = h

h = 60m
